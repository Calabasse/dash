Estimated Serum / Active Load

Model: each administration contributes a curve from the compound's ester half-life; the plot sums them (superposition), same approach as steroidplotter. "Active mg in system" is a relative estimate of hormone released, not a measured ng/dL serum value (true serum needs volume-of-distribution + individual clearance).

Bloodwork & Hormone Panel Total-T calibrates the curve · others logged with ranges

Gyno-risk markers (E2 / Prolactin / Progesterone) are flagged when high. Tren doesn't aromatize — its gyno risk is progestogenic + prolactin, not E2, and a progesterone serum test won't reflect it. Only Total-T scales the serum curve (Tren/Deca/EQ don't assay as testosterone). Switch Units → "ng/dL" to view the calibrated curve.
